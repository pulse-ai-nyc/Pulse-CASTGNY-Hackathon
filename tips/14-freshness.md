# Tip 14: Keep Content Fresh

**What it is:** How recently the brand's key pages were updated — signaled by publish dates, last-modified headers, and content recency.

**How to measure it:**
- Check last-modified dates on key pages (HTTP headers or on-page dates)
- Look for publish/update dates in blog posts, news, and FAQ pages
- Compare to competitors: are their pages more recently updated?

**Example:**
> Your brand's "Programs" page was last updated in 2022  
> Competitor's equivalent page says "Updated January 2026"  
> → Models prefer fresher content because it's more likely to be accurate

**Why it matters:**
AI models and search engines both favor recent content. A stale page signals that the information may be outdated, making models less confident in citing it. Regular updates — even small ones — signal that the brand is active and the content is trustworthy.

**What your agent should do:**
- Report the age of key pages (last-modified dates)
- Flag pages older than 6–12 months as stale
- Recommend a content refresh cadence (e.g. "Update your top 5 pages quarterly")

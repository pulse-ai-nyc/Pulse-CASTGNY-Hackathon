# Tip 10: Earn Backlinks & Domain Authority

**What it is:** The number and quality of external websites linking to the brand's site — a core signal of trustworthiness for both search engines and AI models.

**How to measure it:**
- Backlink count and referring domains (via APIs like Moz, Ahrefs, or free alternatives)
- Presence on high-authority sites: news outlets, Wikipedia, .edu, .gov
- Compare to competitors' backlink profiles

**Example:**
> Competitor: 2,400 backlinks from 180 referring domains, including Forbes and Wikipedia  
> Your brand: 120 backlinks from 30 domains, no major publications  
> → Authority gap explains why the competitor is recommended by AI models

**Why it matters:**
AI models (and the search engines that feed them) treat backlinks as votes of confidence. More high-quality links = more trust = more likely to be mentioned and cited. This is the hardest lever to move, but also one of the most impactful.

**What your agent should do:**
- Report backlink counts for the brand and top competitors
- Highlight authority gaps (e.g. "Competitor has 3 .edu links; you have 0")
- Recommend link-building strategies: guest posts, partnerships, PR, research publications
